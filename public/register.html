Be the Cloud.

StarDust is a personal web application platform. Whether you're a user, developer, community leader, entreprenuer, or oliphaunt, you can run a StarDust.

Before StarDust you only ever installed apps locally and the cloud components were somewhere else.

With StarDust you have complete control over apps. This means you control your data.

Flexible

StarDust is the framework around which other services and applications are built. Services can do things like pull your data out of social networks. Applications could use that data (and perhaps other types of data, e.g. your credit card transactions) to craft a more personal social experience.

Secure

StarDust is a server that you or your community control. This means that your data likely won't be used in some way you're uncomfortable with.

StarDust is designed from the ground up to support Content Addressible Data and Link Contacts. This means that each piece of data you store in StarDust can be used by other services and applications, but only if given explicit permission by you.

Open

StarDust is completely open source (MIT). No one can own or control the development of the core StarDust platform. Anyone can improve it.

Portable

If you setup your very own StarDust, it's yours. Don't have the technical chops to do that? Join a community. Don't like what that community wants to do with your data? Find another or pay for someone to setup your very own StarDust for you.

Every time you move to another StarDust, you take your data, applications, and services with you. When you move to a new home you don't leave all your furniture behind. Why should digital homes be any different?
